TRIBUTE

A giant in the field of early childhood development, he devoted his life to studying, nurturing and advocating on behalf of babies, toddlers and their families.

Timeline of Dr. Thomas Berry Brazelton:

  • 1918 - Born May 10 in Waco, Texas.
  • 1940 - Graduated from Princeton University.
  • 1943 - Graduated from Columbia University College of Physicians and Surgeons in New York City where he accepted a medical internship at Roosevelt Hospital.
  • 1944 - Served in the U.S. Navy as a shipboard physician.
  • 1945 - Completed his medical residency in Boston Massachusetts General Hospital (MGH). Completed an additional residency in child psychiatry at the James Jackson Putnam Children's Center in Roxbury.
  • 1949 - Married wife of 66 years the former Christina Lowell (2015). They had four surviving children and five grandchildren.
  • 1950 - Opened a pediatric practice in Cambridge, Massachusetts.
  • 1951 - Began a teaching position at Harvard Medical School.
  • 1960s - With Professor Jerome Bruner, he was a Fellow at the Center for Cognitive Studies at Harvard University.
  • 1969 - Authored his first book, "Infants and Mothers".
  • 1972 - Established the Child Development Unit, a pediatric training and research center at Children's Hospital in Boston.
  • 1973 - Developed the Neonatal Behavioral Assessment Scale (NBAS).
  • 1983 - Hosted cable TV “What Every Baby Knows” (1983 - 1996)
  • 1987 - President of the Society for Research in Child Development (1987–1989).
  • 1988 - Clinical Professor of Pediatrics Emeritus at Harvard Medical School. President of the National Center for Clinical Infant Programs (1988–1991).
  • 1989 - Appointed to the National Commission on Children.
  • 1993 - Founded the Brazelton Touchpoints Center at Boston Children’s Hospital.
  • 2000 - Honored as a Living Legend by the United States Library of Congress.
  • 2013 - Awarded the Presidential Citizens Medal by President Barack Obama.
  • 2018 - Dies March 13, at the age of 99 in Barnstable, Massachusetts.
